The Vikings travelled far and wide during the Viking Age (793 AD–1066 AD). The original stories about the Vikings were written up in sagas. The best known is the Heimskringla, with sagas about the Viking kings. The Heimskringla was compiled by the Icelander Snorri Sturluson around 1230 AD). But how well do you actually know your Vikings?

In this light-hearted game you will be shown an image or question linked to the Vikings, and your task is to match the fact (or Viking) to the related tile on a grid. Some tiles have written facts, and some have illustrations.

If you think you know the Vikings, then go ahead and click Start!

Instructions how to play the Viking Facts Game

The game is very simple. The object is to match the correct tile from the 9 options on the right to the Viking or Fact given on the left. You have 3 lives, and lose 1 life for each wrong answer, until you have no lives left and the game ends. Every right answer gives you 1 point, and if you score 9 points you are officially fit to be a Viking!

Rollo of Normandy

(year of birth unknown, died between 928 and 933)

Rollo was a Viking who conducted raids on the coast of France starting in the 800s. Scholars are unsure whether Rollo was of Danish or Norwegian origin.

Following his early raids on France, Rollo also pillaged and led expeditions in England, Scotland, Ireland, and Flanders. Rollo’s continual raids on France led Charles the Simple, king of the Franks, to give the Viking leader the region of Normandy.

The Treaty of Saint Claire-sur-Epte made Rollo the first duke of Normandy.

Olav Haraldsson

(c.995 - 1030)

Olav Haraldsson, was the first Viking king to unify all of Norway (which had previously been made small kingdoms).

He went on his first Viking raid when he was 12 years old, and raided far and wide around Europe. He famously caused the collapse of London Bridge during a raid in England (hence the much-loved song 'London Bridge is falling down').

Olav was a Christian, and was the first king to convert Norway to Christianity from the pagan Viking faith. He is known as St. Olav, the patron saint of Norway.

Leif Erikson

(c.970s - c.1019 to 1025)

As claims to fame go, Leif Erikson's isn't half bad. Leif is generally considered to have been the first European to set foot in North America, a full 500 years before Christopher Columbus.

The son of Erik the Red, Leif is thought to have arrived in the New World in around 1000, having ventured off course en route to Greenland. His crew set up camp in a place he dubbed “Vinland”, thought to be Newfoundland.

Leif is celebrated over large parts of the United States as the first European settler in America.

Harald Hardrada

(c.1015 - 1066)

Harald Hardrada is known as 'The Last Great Viking'. Harald became known as an adventurer, mercenary, king, conqueror, administrator and hero of the Icelandic sagas.

At the time of his birth, Norway was part of the Danish empire of King Cnut the Great, which included England and parts of Sweden. Harald travelled and raided in Europe and the Middle East (he lived in Constantinople for a few years and was head of the Varngian Guards, the bodyguards of the Roman Emperor).

After Cnut died, he wanted to take the throne, and sailed to England to do so. There he died at the battle of Stamford Bridge in 1066.

Cnut the Great

(955 - 1035)

As the conqueror of England, Denmark, and Norway, Cnut is one of the most influential Vikings in history, establishing a Norse rule in the North Sea for the first and only time in history.

In the summer of 1015, Cnut's fleet set sail for England with a Danish army of perhaps 10,000 in 200 longships. Cnut ruled England for nearly two decades, and a big factor in why he stayed on the throne for so long was that Vikings were no longer a threat to the population!

Cnut famously once tried to order the tide back from the shore, and when the tide still came in as usual acclaimed that no earthly king could compare with God.
